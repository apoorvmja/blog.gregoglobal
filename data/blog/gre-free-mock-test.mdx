## Frequently Asked Questions about the GRE

1. **What is the GRE, and who should take it?**
   - The GRE is a standardized test required for admission to many graduate schools. It’s ideal for students aiming to pursue a master's degree or PhD.

2. **How often can I take the GRE?**
   - The GRE can be taken once every 21 days, up to five times within a 12-month period.

3. **What is a good GRE score?**
   - A good score varies by program, but generally, a score above 300 is considered competitive.

4. **How long should I study for the GRE?**
   - Most students benefit from a 6 to 12-week study plan, depending on their baseline knowledge and target score.

5. **Are there any penalties for wrong answers on the GRE?**
   - No, there is no penalty for wrong answers—it's beneficial to attempt every question.

6. **How can I improve my verbal reasoning score?**
   - Focus on vocabulary, reading comprehension, and regular practice.

7. **What math concepts should I focus on for the GRE?**
   - Key areas include algebra, geometry, data analysis, and basic arithmetic.

8. **Can I use a calculator on the GRE?**
   - A calculator is available for the quant sections, but is not allowed for the analytical writing section.

9. **What should I bring to the GRE test center?**
   - Bring your identification and any required documents, as specified by the testing agency.

10. **How do I send my GRE scores to universities?**
    - You can send your scores as part of your application through ETS’s official score reporting service.
