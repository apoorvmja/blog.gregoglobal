## Types of GRE Tests

1. **GRE General Test**: This is the most commonly taken GRE and assesses verbal reasoning, quantitative reasoning, and analytical writing.
   
2. **GRE Subject Tests**: These tests are available for specific subjects such as psychology, biology, chemistry, and literature. These exams are perfect for those who want to showcase their expertise in a particular field.

> The GRE General Test is often recommended for most graduate programs, while subject tests can provide additional value in specialized disciplines.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Here are answers to some common questions surrounding the GRE:

1. **What is the GRE?**
   - The GRE is a standardized test for admission to many graduate schools, assessing various aspects of your academic readiness.
   
2. **How often can I take the GRE?**
   - You can take the GRE once every 21 days, and up to five times within any continuous rolling 12-month period.
   
3. **What is a good GRE score?**
   - A good score varies by program, but generally, a score of 300 or above is considered competitive.
   
4. **How long should I study for the GRE?**
   - The duration of study varies, but 2-3 months with regular practice is ideal for most students.
   
5. **Are there penalties for wrong answers?**
   - No, there are no penalties for guessing, so it's best to answer every question to maximize your score.

6. **How can I improve my verbal reasoning score?**
   - Enhance your vocabulary and practice reading complex texts, as well as studying word roots, prefixes, and suffixes.
   
7. **What math concepts should I focus on for the GRE?**
   - Key concepts include arithmetic, algebra, and geometry. Familiarize yourself with the question formats to enhance your strategies.

8. **Can I use a calculator?**
   - A calculator is only allowed for the Quantitative Reasoning section, and it will be provided on-screen.

9. **What should I bring to the GRE test center?**
   - You should have your identification (ID), confirmation printout, and a snack for breaks.

10. **How do I send my GRE scores to universities?**
    - You can choose to send your scores to universities directly after taking the test using the ETS ScoreSelect option.
