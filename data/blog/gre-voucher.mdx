## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. **What is the GRE, and who should take it?**

   - The GRE is a standardized test that evaluates graduate school readiness. It’s required by many graduate programs.

2. **How often can I take the GRE?**

   - You can take the GRE once every 21 days, up to five times within a 12-month period.

3. **What is a good GRE score?**

   - A good score varies by program but generally, a combined score of 300 or higher is considered competitive.

4. **How long should I study for the GRE?**

   - Most students study between 4 to 12 weeks, depending on their baseline readiness and goals.

5. **Are there any penalties for wrong answers on the GRE?**

   - No, there is no penalty for wrong answers. You should try to answer every question.

6. **How can I improve my verbal reasoning score?**

   - Focus on reading comprehension and vocabulary; practice with high-quality GRE materials.

7. **What math concepts should I focus on for the GRE?**

   - Prioritize arithmetic, algebra, and geometry concepts, and review data interpretation as well.

8. **Can I use a calculator on the GRE?**

   - You can only use a calculator for the Quantitative Reasoning section. It will be provided on screen.

9. **What should I bring to the GRE test center?**

   - A valid photo ID, confirmation email, and any required materials as specified by the GRE guidelines.

10. **How do I send my GRE scores to universities?**

- You'll have the option to select schools during the test or send scores afterward through your ETS account.
